Pillar Deep Dives
Pillar 01
Discoverability 14/25
Weak — NAP Inconsistency
Findings
  • Website live at jcamgraphics.com — meta title targets "Custom Car Vinyl Wraps & Sign Printing Louisville, KY" — adequate keyword intent but no competitive differentiation
  • Meta description is thin and repetitive — keyword stuffing with no value proposition = below-average organic CTR
  • NAP mismatch across platforms — "JCGI" on site/Facebook vs. "JCAM Graphics" on domain/Yelp — Google treats this as a trust failure and suppresses local pack placement
  • No blog, no content marketing — zero owned search real estate for high-intent queries like "fleet wrap cost Louisville"
  • No service-area pages for Lexington, Cincinnati, or Nashville corridor = missed commercial fleet geography
Pillar 02
Trust Signals 5/25
Critical — Zero Social Proof
Findings
  • No on-site testimonials or reviews — zero peer validation; cold prospects have no reason to choose JCGI over a competitor with 100+ Google reviews
  • Facebook: "Not yet rated" — 4 total reviews — a 30-year-old business in 2026 with 4 Facebook reviews is self-eliminating from social-proof-driven shortlists
  • No Google star rating surfaced — either the GBP is unclaimed or has <5 reviews; both outcomes suppress local visibility entirely
  • No named case studies — gallery shows work but names no client, vehicle count, or outcome; enterprise fleet buyers purchase on proof at scale
  • Certifications buried — 3M, PDAA, UASG, Second Skin are listed but not visually prominent; these differentiate JCGI from every unlicensed competitor
  • Industry data: businesses with 25+ reviews receive 108% more calls; GBP with 20+ photos get 42% more direction requests
Potential revenue impact of fixing Trust Signals: $10,000–$48,000/year
